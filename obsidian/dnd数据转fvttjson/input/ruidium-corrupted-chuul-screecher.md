## Traits

- **Amphibious**. The screecher can breathe air and water.
- **Sense Magic**. The screecher can sense magic within 120 feet as if it had the *detect magic* spell cast on it, except this trait isn't magical.
- **Brittle Ruidium Shell**. If the screecher takes 15 or more bludgeoning damage from a single source, its AC becomes 14 until it finishes a short or long rest. While its AC is reduced this way, it loses thunder resistance.
- **Bloodied Crescendo**. The first time the screecher is reduced to 57 HP or fewer, it immediately recharges Ruidium Screech. The next time it uses Ruidium Screech, the area increases to a 60-foot cone instead of a 30-foot cone.
- **Ruidium Corruption**. When a creature is required to make a Ruidium Corruption save due to a trait or action of this screecher, the creature must succeed on a DC 15 Charisma saving throw or suffer Ruidium Corruption. If the creature is already affected by Ruidium Corruption, it instead gains 1 level of exhaustion.

## Actions

- **Multiattack**. The screecher makes two Rending Claw attacks. If it's grappling a creature, it can replace one of these attacks with a Tentacles attack.
- **Rending Claw**. *Melee Weapon Attack*: +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit*: 13 (2d8 + 4) slashing damage plus 3 (1d6) thunder damage. If the target is Large or smaller, it's grappled (escape DC 14). The screecher can have only one creature grappled this way at a time.
- **Tentacles**. A creature grappled by the screecher must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned until the end of its next turn. While poisoned this way, attacks against it score a critical hit on a roll of 17–20. If the target is already affected by Ruidium Corruption, it instead becomes paralyzed until the end of its next turn.
- **Ruidium Screech** (Recharge 5-6). The screecher releases a deafening screech infused with ruidium resonance. Each creature in a 30-foot cone must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or take 18 (4d8) thunder damage plus 7 (2d6) psychic damage and be deafened and unable to take reactions until the end of the screecher's next turn. If it's concentrating, it must immediately make a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration with disadvantage. If the screecher is bloodied and the target fails its save, the target must also make a Ruidium Corruption save. On a successful save, it takes half damage with no other effects.
