## Traits

- **Amphibious**. The nullifier can breathe air and water.
- **Sense Magic**. The nullifier can sense magic within 120 feet as if it had the *detect magic* spell cast on it, except this trait isn't magical.
- **Brittle Ruidium Shell**. If the nullifier takes 20 or more bludgeoning damage from a single source, its AC becomes 15 until it finishes a short or long rest. While its AC is reduced this way, it loses psychic resistance.
- **Nullifying Field**. The nullifier emits a nullifying field that suppresses magic in a 10-foot radius. A creature in that area that is concentrating has disadvantage on Constitution saving throws to maintain concentration. In addition, when a creature in that area casts a spell of 1st level or higher, or activates a magic item effect that deals damage, restores HP, teleports, or imposes a condition, it must first succeed on a DC 15 Spellcasting ability check or the effect fails, but the spell slot, charge, or other resource is still expended.
- **Ruidium Corruption**. When a creature is required to make a Ruidium Corruption save due to a trait or action of this nullifier, the creature must succeed on a DC 15 Charisma saving throw or suffer Ruidium Corruption. If the creature is already affected by Ruidium Corruption, it instead gains 1 level of exhaustion.

## Actions

- **Multiattack**. The nullifier makes one Seizing Claw attack and one Crushing Claw attack. If it's grappling a creature, it can also make one Tentacles attack.
- **Seizing Claw**. *Melee Weapon Attack*: +8 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target. *Hit*: 14 (2d8 + 5) bludgeoning damage plus 4 (1d8) psychic damage. If the target is Large or smaller, it's grappled (escape DC 16) and pulled up to 10 feet toward the nullifier. The nullifier can have only one creature grappled this way at a time.
- **Crushing Claw**. *Melee Weapon Attack*: +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. *Hit*: 18 (3d8 + 5) bludgeoning damage plus 7 (2d6) psychic damage. The target must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or can't take reactions until the start of the nullifier's next turn. If it's concentrating, it must immediately make a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration with disadvantage.
- **Tentacles**. A creature grappled by the nullifier must succeed on a DC 15 Constitution saving throw or be poisoned and deafened until the end of its next turn. While poisoned this way, it has disadvantage on Constitution saving throws to maintain concentration. If the target is already affected by Ruidium Corruption, it instead becomes paralyzed until the end of its next turn.

## Bonus Actions

- **Antimagic Pulse** (Recharge 5-6). The nullifier compresses its nullifying field into a brief antimagic field. Until the start of its next turn, the nullifier and creatures within 5 feet of it are in an antimagic area. If the nullifier is bloodied (72 HP or fewer), the area increases to 10 feet.

## Reactions

- **Null Collapse** (1/day). The first time the nullifier is reduced to 72 HP or fewer, it can immediately release a wave of collapsing null energy. Until the end of its next turn, its Nullifying Field radius increases to 20 feet. Creatures of the nullifier's choice in that area must succeed on a DC 15 Intelligence saving throw or take 22 (4d10) psychic damage and can't take reactions until the end of their next turn, and if they're concentrating, their concentration immediately ends. The creature must also make a Ruidium Corruption save. On a successful save, it takes half damage with no other effects.
